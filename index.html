Research

My work is in the Arias Group, led by Professor Tomas Arias, where we use ab initio quantum mechanical calculations to explore how complex material behaviors emerge from the interactions of electrons and nuclei. This involves developing and applying computational methods to study phenomena across different length and time scales.

Current projects include investigating phonon dynamics in low-dimensional systems, surface interactions, and the quantum mechanics of materials in contact with solutions. These studies contribute to understanding mechanical properties, crystal growth processes, and potential applications in energy technologies like solar cells and fuel cells.

Our research employs density functional theory (DFT) and beyond-DFT methods to accurately model electronic structure and dynamics. We develop novel computational algorithms to handle large-scale simulations of complex systems, including those with defects, interfaces, and under external conditions like pressure or electric fields.

The interdisciplinary nature of our work bridges fundamental physics with practical applications, from designing new materials for energy storage to understanding biological systems at the molecular level. We collaborate with experimental groups to validate our theoretical predictions and guide future experiments.

Key techniques we use include:

  • Ab initio molecular dynamics simulations
  • Phonon calculations and thermal transport
  • Surface and interface modeling
  • Quantum chemistry methods
  • Machine learning for materials discovery

These computational tools allow us to predict material properties before synthesis, saving time and resources in the development of new technologies.
